The three-pillar frame is already the right shape.

Tavira Group helps SMEs and non-profits scale by reducing operational cost and optimizing workflows. The three-pillar frame (automation, access to money, cost reductions) is already the right shape for the mid-market. The question is how to run that frame at more than one client at a time without rebuilding scoping from scratch every engagement.

The 60 / 30 / 10 working rule.

Most automation projects waste budget by putting a language model where a database would do the job faster, cheaper, and with stronger guarantees. The working rule is roughly 60% traditional code and data, 30% rule-based logic, 10% genuine AI.

60%
Traditional code & data
Databases, pipelines, reports. Boring on the deck, load-bearing in delivery.
30%
Rule-based logic
Forms, eligibility, routing, documentation flows. Deterministic where the law or policy already is.
10%
Genuine AI
Language, unstructured text, judgment-assisted steps. Where a model actually earns its keep.

Applied to your three pillars

Access to money (tax credits, rebates) MAPS TO Largely rule-based. Forms, eligibility, documentation.
Cost reductions (payroll, energy, payments, shipping) MAPS TO Mostly data & comparison work. Benchmark, renegotiate, track.
Hyperautomation (BPA & AI) MAPS TO Where AI belongs, and where a published context architecture turns bespoke discovery into a repeatable artifact a team can run.
